<p><p>Multiaxial fabrics are categorized into different types based on the number of axis they possess. Bidirectional fabrics have two axis, providing strength in two directions. Triaxial fabrics have three axis, offering strength in three directions. Quadraxial fabrics have four axis, giving strength in four directions. Other types of multiaxial fabrics may have five or more axis, providing strength in multiple directions. Each type of fabric offers specific properties and is used in various applications based on the required directional strength.</p></p>
