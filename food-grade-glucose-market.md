<p><p>Food Grade Glucose market is segmented into two types: Monohydrate Glucose and Anhydrous Glucose. Monohydrate Glucose is a form of glucose that contains one molecule of water, while Anhydrous Glucose is a form of glucose that does not contain any water molecules. Monohydrate Glucose is commonly used in food and beverage industry for its sweetening and thickening properties, while Anhydrous Glucose is preferred in applications where water content needs to be minimized, such as confectionery and baked goods.</p></p>
