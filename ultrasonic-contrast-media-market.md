<p><p>Ultrasonic contrast media are substances used to enhance ultrasound images for better visibility of internal organs and blood vessels. Two common types are iodine-based contrast media, which are often used in cardiac and vascular imaging, and barium sulfate compounds, which are commonly used in gastrointestinal imaging. Iodine-based contrast media are preferred for their high visibility in blood vessels, while barium sulfate compounds are ideal for highlighting the digestive tract due to their higher density and compatibility with X-rays.</p></p>
