<p><p>Ultrasound Non-destructive Testing (NDT) is a method used to evaluate the integrity of materials or structures without causing any damage. Various types of NDT methods include ultrasonic testing, radiography testing, visual inspection testing, magnetic particle testing, electromagnetic testing, eddy-current testing, liquid penetrant testing, and others. Ultrasonic testing uses high-frequency sound waves to detect flaws or measure thickness. Radiography testing involves using radiation to image the internal structure of an object. Visual inspection testing involves physically looking for defects. Magnetic particle testing uses magnetic fields to detect surface and near-surface flaws. Eddy-current testing measures changes in electrical currents induced in a conductive material to detect flaws. Liquid penetrant testing involves applying a liquid dye to an object's surface to detect surface-breaking flaws. Other NDT methods include radiographic testing, acoustic emission testing, and infrared testing, among others.</p></p>
