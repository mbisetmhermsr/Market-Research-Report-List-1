<p><p>Amphibious vehicles are vehicles that can operate both on land and in water. There are four main types of amphibious vehicles on the market: screw propeller, water jet, track-based, and others. Screw propeller vehicles use a propeller for propulsion in water. Water jet vehicles use jet streams of water for propulsion. Track-based vehicles have tracks like a tank for movement on land and in water. Other types may include air cushion vehicles or hovercrafts. Each type has its own advantages and limitations in terms of speed, maneuverability, and terrain capabilities.</p></p>
