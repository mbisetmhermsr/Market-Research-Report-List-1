<p><p>Harbour tugboats are commonly classified into two main market types: deep sea tugboats and river tugboats. Deep sea tugboats are designed for longer journeys and heavy-duty towing operations in open waters, making them suitable for assisting large ships in harbours or out at sea. River tugboats, on the other hand, are specifically built for navigating inland waterways and shallow rivers, where they can provide towing and maneuvering services for smaller vessels in narrower and more confined spaces. Both types play crucial roles in the maritime industry by ensuring safe and efficient operations for vessels of varying sizes.</p></p>
